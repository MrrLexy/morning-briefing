Where we are: IG OAS at 77bps (cycle tights), HY at 286bps (well inside norms), EMBI at 153bps, Fed on hold with a fractured 8-4 vote, and now a geopolitical supply shock via Strait of Hormuz. Risk assets are priced for a soft landing while the macro is delivering a curveball.

Closest parallel — Q3 2019: Fed on hold after a cutting cycle, IG ~90bps, HY ~350bps, and the Abqaiq facility attack in September 2019 spiked WTI ~15% in a session. Credit spreads widened 10–15bps across IG and HY before recovering within 3 weeks as the supply disruption proved temporary. The key lesson: geopolitically-driven credit widening tends to be sharp but short unless it triggers a recession. If the Strait of Hormuz remains partially disrupted for 2–4 weeks, expect IG to widen 10–20bps from current levels before stabilizing. This is calibration, not a prediction.
