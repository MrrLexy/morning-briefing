Team View
We remain in the high-quality carry camp — the rate cycle is on pause and credit fundamentals are sound, but both duration and credit are near full valuation. IG OAS at 80bps requires disciplined issue selection to avoid being the last buyer at the tight. Our preference is intermediate IG (5–7yr) with a financials overweight, CLO AAA for spread pickup with structural protection, and a slight duration short at the long end where fiscal supply is the durable headwind. Benign PCE today extends the constructive backdrop; a hot print forces a full portfolio review. [~]
Historical Parallel

Closest analog: Q2 2018 — Fed on hold after a hiking cycle, IG spreads near 100bps (fair value), curve in late-cycle flattening ahead of steepening reversal. In that environment, IG carry outperformed duration plays by ~120bps over six months. Key difference today: the 30Y fiscal premium at 5.06% was not a structural constraint in 2018 — the long-end supply dynamic makes this cycle more challenging for duration adds, reinforcing the intermediate preference. Alpha Idea
Observation BB HY at 278bps / 3.8yr = 73bps/yr of carry. IG at 80bps / 7.2yr = 11bps/yr. BB/IG carry-per-duration ratio of 6.6× is historically elevated — implying BB is pricing a default-severity premium that current fundamentals do not support. [~]
Evidence (1) BB/IG spread ratio: 278/80 = 3.5× vs. long-run avg ~2.8–3.0×. (2) MLGHYBBH YTD +1.2% vs. LEH_CORP −1.8% = 3.0% outperformance on ~half the duration. (3) BB upgrade pipeline active in Q1 2026 with rising-star candidates in Energy and Consumer Staples. [~]
Risk Hot PCE today forces duration + credit re-correlation — carry advantage can invert quickly if spreads gap wider simultaneously. BB is less liquid than IG in a risk-off move; bid/ask widens materially when dealers pull.
Say it “We’re getting paid 6× as much per year of duration in BB vs. IG right now. That ratio only makes sense if you believe defaults are about to spike materially. We don’t — so size the BB allocation within the HY bucket.”
Question for the PM / Desk Strategist: If PCE prints hot today at +2.9% and the 10Y spikes to 4.65%, does the curve bull-flatten (front end reprices harder — 2Y +12bps, 10Y +8bps) or bear-steepen (long end leads — 10Y +12bps, 2Y +8bps)? Our duration-short is concentrated in the belly (5yr) — a bear-steepener compresses the short-end gains and leaves the long end under pressure regardless. Should we hedge some belly-short with a token 30Y long before 8:30 to reduce P&L asymmetry? The 30Y fiscal premium argues against it structurally, but tactically a bull-flatten after soft PCE could be painful on the 5yr position.
