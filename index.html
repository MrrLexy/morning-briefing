The 5.0–5.5 coupon bucket carries meaningful extension risk as those borrowers are locked well below market; avoid in a rates-higher scenario.

30yr vs. 15yr: 30yr preferred — the +28bps carry premium compensates for additional duration. 15yr is more vulnerable to adverse extension in a further-rates-up scenario.

Prepay outlook: Speeds expected to remain suppressed through at least Q3 2026. With mortgage rates at 6.25%, the refi incentive is zero across most of the outstanding stock. Stable prepayments are a feature for current coupon (6.0–6.5) holders.

Team View: We remain positioned for higher-for-longer rates while harvesting carry in high-quality credit and securitized. The central market tension is the divergence between resilient credit spreads and elevated rates — we believe this resolves via spread widening, not a rates rally, as post-holiday supply pressure and the eventual consumer slowdown from $90+ oil filter into credit risk premium. Conviction stays with carry over duration and quality over yield until the May CPI print on June 10.
Historical Parallel

Closest parallel: Q4 2022 — a bear steepener driven by energy inflation and Fed hike expectations pushed the 10Y to 4.25% and the 30Y to 4.33%. Credit spreads lagged the rate sell-off by 6–8 weeks before IG widened 20–30bps; HY followed with 50–75bps in the subsequent quarter. Today's setup features the same rate/credit divergence at a higher starting level (30Y at 5.12% vs. 4.33%). This is calibration, not prediction — the rate move is arguably more advanced today, which could mean the spread catch-up is closer.
