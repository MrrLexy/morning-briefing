Coupon stack call: The 6.0–6.5 coupon FNMA/GNMA 30yr stack is most attractive today. With 30Y mortgage rates rising to ~6.25%, the refinancing option for 6.0-coupon borrowers is effectively at-the-money-to-out-of-the-money — the 24bp rate spike this week has killed the refi incentive. This removes extension risk and delivers more predictable cash flows. The 5.0–5.5 coupon bucket carries meaningful extension risk as those borrowers are locked well below current market rates and will never refinance — duration grows with every rate move higher. Prefer FNMA 6.0–6.5, avoid FNMA 5.0–5.5 in a rates-higher scenario.

30yr vs. 15yr: 30yr preferred. The +28bps carry premium compensates for the additional duration given our neutral-to-short duration posture via other book adjustments. 15yr MBS is more vulnerable to adverse extension in a further-rates-up scenario.

Prepayment outlook: Speeds expected to remain suppressed through at least Q3 2026. With mortgage rates at 6.25% and the average existing mortgage rate well below market, the refin incentive is zero across most of the outstanding stock. Stable prepayments are a feature for current coupon (6.0–6.5) holders.

Fed QT: The Fed’s ~$2.7T MBS portfolio continues running off at approximately $15–17B/month [~] — a structural technical headwind on spread levels that explains why OAS remains compressed despite the underlying credit and duration quality of Agency paper.

The take: Prefer 6.0–6.5 coupon 30yr FNMA/GNMA — rate spike killed the refi option, stabilizing cash flows; 148bps nominal spread to the 10Y represents compelling carry vs. equivalent IG duration risk.

Risks to Monitor

  1. Oil-driven inflation persistence — WTI above $90 sustaining into Q3 pushes core PCE to 3.5%+, forcing the Fed to hike; 2Y UST toward 4.50%, IG spreads widen 15–25bps to catch up with rates.
  2. 30Y supply / fiscal risk premium expansion — The 30Y at 5.12% already reflects meaningful term premium; any deterioration in the fiscal picture (spending legislation, debt ceiling) adds another leg — duration positioning risk is asymmetric to the upside.
  3. Credit spread catch-up — IG at 80bps and HY at 286bps are historically tight for a no-cut environment; this divergence from rates has persisted longer than expected but does not resolve itself — the reversion typically comes quickly and via new issue concession pressure, not slowly via secondary widening.
Team View: We remain positioned for higher-for-longer rates while harvesting carry in high-quality credit and securitized. The central market tension is the divergence between resilient credit spreads and an elevated rate regime — we believe this resolves via spread widening (not a rates rally) as post-holiday supply pressure and the eventual consumer/growth slowdown from $90+ oil filter into credit risk premium. Conviction stays with carry over duration and quality over yield until the May CPI print on June 10 resets the narrative.

Historical Parallel

Closest parallel: Q4 2022 — a bear steepener driven by energy inflation and Fed hike expectations pushed the 10Y to 4.25% and the 30Y to 4.33%. Credit spreads lagged the rate sell-off by 6–8 weeks before IG widened 20–30bps; HY followed with a 50–75bps widening in the subsequent quarter. Today’s setup features the same rate/credit divergence at a higher starting level (30Y at 5.12% vs. 4.33%). This is calibration, not prediction — the rate move is arguably more advanced today, which could mean the spread catch-up is closer.

ObservationThe 30Y Treasury at 5.12% is the best entry point in the long bond in nearly 20 years. With breakeven inflation near 2.7%, the implied real yield is ~2.4% — historically associated with subsequent bond market rallies once the inflation impulse fades.
Evidence(1) 30Y real yields above 2.0% have historically preceded 12-month total returns of 8–15% in long Treasuries; (2) Oil shocks reduce consumer spending within 2–3 quarters — disinflationary lag; (3) The Fed is already in restrictive territory at 3.75% real — each additional month at this level slows credit creation further
RiskFiscal deterioration is structural, not cyclical. If the market continues to rerate the terminal rate above 4.0% or expands the fiscal risk premium, the 30Y pushes to 5.50%+ and the entry-point thesis fails on near-term mark-to-market. This is a position for accounts with duration mandate and multi-quarter horizon.
